# Cora - User Personas

## Overview

These personas represent the core user types Cora is built for. Each has different motivations, behaviours and needs — but they all share a common thread: they want to challenge themselves and the people around them in a way that existing apps do not support well.

---

## Persona 1 - The Social Challenger

**Name:** Jake, 24  
**Occupation:** Tradie  
**Location:** Sydney, Australia

### About
Jake and his mates are always competing. Whether it is who can hit the most kms in a month, who can stick to a diet the longest, or who can do the most pushups in a week. There is always something going on in the group chat. He does not take it too seriously but the banter and accountability keep him showing up.

He tried to run a 3-month running competition with his friends tracking kilometres at a minimum pace. Someone in the group built a Strava API scraper and used Excel to track the data. It worked, but it was a nightmare to manage. He just wants something that handles it automatically and lets everyone see the leaderboard without a developer friend having to maintain a spreadsheet.

### Goals
- Compete with friends in a fun, low-pressure way
- See everyone's progress in one place without chasing people for updates
- Give and receive encouragement; the banter is part of the experience

### Pain Points
- No app supports custom group challenges with flexible rules
- Cobbling together Strava, Excel and group chats is messy and unsustainable
- Loses interest when there is no social layer keeping him engaged

### How He Uses Cora
- Creates or joins group challenges with mates
- Fuels his friends' posts and reminds them loudly when they haven't fuelled his
- Checks the group feed daily to see who is falling behind

### Key Quote
"Bro give me some fuel on my last post."

## Persona 2 - The Community Joiner

**Name:** Priya, 28  
**Occupation:** Marketing Coordinator  
**Location:** Melbourne, Australia

### About
Priya discovered 75 Hard on Instagram and wanted to try it but did not know anyone doing it. She finds motivation in being part of something bigger, knowing that thousands of other people are on the same journey makes her feel less alone. She is not necessarily competitive but she wants to feel connected to a community that shares her values around discipline and self-improvement.

### Goals
- Find and join popular challenges that others are already doing
- Feel part of a community even when doing a challenge solo
- Share her progress publicly and receive encouragement from strangers and friends alike

### Pain Points
- Existing apps do not connect her to others doing the same challenge
- Social media is too noisy and she wants a focused space for progress not likes on selfies
- Hard to find challenges that are structured and proven rather than making something up herself

### How She Uses Cora
- Browses community challenges and filters by category
- Starts a named instance of a popular template: "Priya's 2026 75 Hard"
- Posts public proof submissions and engages with others doing the same challenge
- Follows users who inspire her and fuels their posts regularly

### Key Quote
"I just want to know other people are doing this too."

## Persona 3 - The Structure Seeker

**Name:** Marcus, 31  
**Occupation:** Software Developer  
**Location:** Brisbane, Australia

### About
Marcus is already into self-improvement. He reads books on habit formation, tracks his workouts in a spreadsheet, and has tried every productivity app on the market. He loves systems. What he wants is a platform flexible enough to support his own custom challenges with specific requirements, proof types and frequencies, not a rigid template someone else designed.

He would be one of Cora's power users, creating detailed challenges that end up becoming community templates others adopt.

### Goals
- Build highly customised challenges with specific requirements and proof types
- Track his own progress with precision and review his history over time
- Contribute to the community by publishing well-designed challenge templates

### Pain Points
- Most apps are too rigid. You can only do their challenges, not design your own
- No way to combine multiple requirement types in one challenge
- Progress history is usually locked behind paywalls or poorly visualised

### How He Uses Cora
- Creates detailed challenge templates with multiple requirements, mixed proof types and specific frequencies
- Publishes templates to the community and watches the instance count grow
- Uses the journal proof type extensively for personal reflection
- Checks his completed challenge history and streak records on his profile

### Key Quote
"I want to build the challenge myself, not just pick one off a shelf."

## Persona 4 - The Accountable Self-Improver

**Name:** Sarah, 26  
**Occupation:** Nurse  
**Location:** Perth, Australia

### About
Sarah wants to be better but struggles with consistency when there is no external pressure. She has started and abandoned many challenges on her own. The gym streak that lasted two weeks, the journaling habit that faded after a month. She does not necessarily want a public audience but she needs something to commit to, a record she has to update, proof she has to submit. The act of logging progress is what keeps her going.

She is also cautious about sharing publicly. She does not want people to see her fail. She would rather complete a private challenge and choose to share the journey after the fact once she has actually done it.

### Goals
- Stay accountable to herself without the pressure of a public audience
- Build habits through consistent proof submission even if no one else sees it
- Celebrate completion privately or choose to share the full journey once finished

### Pain Points
- Public social media adds anxiety rather than motivation; fear of being judged for failing
- No app lets her track privately and then share selectively on completion
- Hard to stay consistent without some kind of external commitment mechanism

### How She Uses Cora
- Runs private challenge instances with no public visibility
- Uses journal and photo proof types for personal accountability
- Completes the challenge and uses the go public flow to selectively share her journey
- Follows a few close friends and engages quietly with their progress

### Key Quote
"I do not want anyone watching me fail. But I want everyone to see when I finish."
